# Promoting Your Plugin

The channels below are the accepted ways to raise community awareness of a new plugin.
In all of them, keep announcements informative rather than promotional — focus on what users
can accomplish, not on marketing the product.

Before posting, review each channel's own posting rules. Posts that are off-topic, excessively
self-promotional, or not in English (where required) may be moderated.

---

## Mailing lists

QGIS maintains several mailing lists. The two most relevant for plugin announcements are:

- [qgis-user](https://lists.osgeo.org/mailman/listinfo/qgis-user) — general user discussion;
  the right place to announce a newly published plugin to the broader community.
- [qgis-developer](https://lists.osgeo.org/mailman/listinfo/qgis-developer) — technical
  developer discussion; appropriate when you want peer feedback on implementation choices or
  are looking for contributors.

See the full list at
[qgis.org/community/organisation/mailinglists](https://qgis.org/community/organisation/mailinglists).

## Discourse forum

The QGIS community forum runs on OSGeo-hosted Discourse at
[discourse.osgeo.org/tag/qgis](https://discourse.osgeo.org/tag/qgis).
It is a good place to start a discussion, gather early user feedback, and answer questions
from users who find your plugin.

## GIS Stack Exchange

[GIS Stack Exchange](https://gis.stackexchange.com/?tags=qgis) is an active question-and-answer
site heavily used by QGIS users. Answering relevant questions and mentioning your plugin where
it genuinely helps is a well-accepted community practice.

## Telegram

The official English-spoken QGIS community chat group is available on
[Telegram](https://t.me/joinchat/Aq2V5RPoxYYhXqUPoxRWPQ).
It is an informal, real-time channel suitable for short announcements and quick feedback.

## Matrix / IRC

QGIS has a Matrix room at [#qgis:osgeo.org](https://matrix.to/#/#qgis:osgeo.org), which is
bridged to the `#qgis` channel on [Libera.Chat IRC](https://web.libera.chat/?channels=#qgis).

## Reddit — r/QGIS

The [r/QGIS subreddit](https://www.reddit.com/r/QGIS/) is an active community where plugin
announcements are well received, provided they include a description of what the plugin does
and how to get started.

## Facebook

The English-speaking global QGIS community group on Facebook can be found at
[QGIS Community Official Virtual Group](https://www.facebook.com/groups/qgiscommunityofficialvirtualgroup).

## Local user groups

QGIS has an active network of local and regional user groups. Presenting your plugin to a
relevant user group is a great way to reach domain-specific users and collect focused feedback.
Find groups at [qgis.org/community/groups](https://qgis.org/community/groups/).

## QGIS Open Day (QOD)

The [QGIS Open Day](https://github.com/qgis/QGIS/wiki/QOD-Events) is a monthly informal
virtual meeting held on the last Friday of each month. Sessions are community-driven,
live-streamed, and recorded on the
[QGIS YouTube channel](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCGS162t4hkOA0b35ucf1yng).
It is the ideal venue for a short live demo of a new plugin to a diverse, international audience.
Add a session to the relevant event's wiki page or contact the event organisers to schedule a slot.
